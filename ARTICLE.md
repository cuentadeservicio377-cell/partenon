# THE PARTENON

A working headquarters for Hermes, inside a small business.

Built for the Nous Research × NVIDIA × Stripe hackathon. Open source. Live.

[hermespartenon.online](https://hermespartenon.online/)



Most AI tools today are oracles. You ask, they answer. Then you go back to your spreadsheet and your inbox and do the work yourself.

Hermes is different. Hermes is an agent. It can act.

But an agent without a structure is just a clever assistant that wanders off.

So I built a home for it. I called it The Partenon.



Hermes is the Greek god of messengers, commerce, and travelers. The Parthenon was the house of the gods. A working headquarters, not a museum.

That is what this project is. A working headquarters for Hermes, inside a small business.

I run a small AI lab in Mexico called WS Capital. We don't build software. We study how people, real people, actually use AI in their day-to-day work. We sit with founders, watch what they do, and figure out which parts of their job can be left to a machine and which parts cannot.

What we see, over and over, is this. Founders do not fail because they lack ideas. They fail because the operations eat them alive. The receipts pile up. The follow-ups get forgotten. The invoices never go out. The deadline passes because nobody remembered who was supposed to do what.

The work that does not require genius still requires time. And time is the one thing a small business owner does not have.

That is where Hermes lives. And that is why it needs a Partenon.



The Partenon is not another chat interface. It is a system of seven Hermes profiles that share one business context. Each one handles a territory the others do not touch.

**The Scribe** reads your receipts and writes them down. Income, expenses, suppliers, the cash flow spreadsheet you abandoned in February. Output goes to Google Sheets, in your Drive, where your accountant can see it.

**The Herald** drafts the copy and the calendar. The LinkedIn post you have been putting off. The pitch deck for the Tuesday client. The newsletter you cancelled three months ago.

**The Collector** turns invoices into actual money. Stripe payment links, subscriptions, reminders that send themselves. You stop chasing clients. They get a link and a date.

**The Guardian** audits the API keys you forgot you had. Recommends which model fits which task. Logs who accessed what. Today it runs on heuristics. When NVIDIA NemoClaw ships, the GPU allocation lands there.

**The Strategist** plans the week. Projects, tasks, the calendar event you double-booked. Morning briefing, midday pulse, end-of-day wrap.

**The Diplomat** keeps the clients and the vendors. Follow-ups, milestones, the proposal you sent and forgot about.

**The Brain** holds the memory. Not the conversation. The decisions, the weekly reviews, the thing you said in January you need to remember in July. Shared across all seven.

Three status tags. **LIVE** runs the moment you install. **CONNECT** works after you add your own Stripe or Google credentials. **ROADMAP** is not built yet, and we say that on every card.

You download the Partenon, configure the heroes you need, and start running missions. Each profile works from the same memory, so handoffs are natural and nothing is lost.

No credentials are required to explore. You stay in control of every live action.

184 tests pass. The installer is idempotent. Docker Compose brings the full stack up on a VPS in about four minutes.

But the hard part is never the code. The hard part is adapting the system to your company. What are your income sources. Who are your suppliers. What projects are actually active. What did you promise a client three weeks ago. Where are the passwords.

That is why I built the  workshop.

The idea is never to leave a great repo and thas it, this is a production ready product, thanks to the Hermes agent framework, the people just need a little help understanding from an expert that speak business and AI. The expert get a room full of people and start working with the group, load the first three profiles with your real data, and configure the Brain to run a weekly review and a morning briefing. the entrepreneurs leave with a system running on their machine with their actual business inside it.

If you do not have your finances organized before the session, we give you a prompt to extract them with any AI assistant. Same for your brand, your clients, your projects, your security inventory. The workshop exists because AI only helps when it knows your business. And your business is in your documents, your calendar, and your memory.


I did not build this for enterprise procurement committees. I built it for the person in the room who actually feels the pain.

The solo founder. The self-employed professional. The person who is the company.

But also for the places where those people gather. Coworking spaces. Business schools. Networking organizations. Chambers of commerce. A coworking space has twenty founders under one roof. A business school has hundreds of future and some current founders. A BNI chapter meets every Thursday with 40 decision-makers already in the room.

These are the perfect places to run installation workshops because the audience is already there.

One founder can run the whole thing on a laptop. A company can run it on a VPS with a team. The architecture is the same.



Every small business has invisible admin work. Someone has to chase invoices. Someone has to remember the follow-up. Someone has to update the cash flow spreadsheet. In most companies that someone is the founder, at 11 PM.

The Partenon is designed to turn that work into a role. A set of profiles that run on a schedule, keep memory across sessions, and hand off work to the right tool. An employee that does not sleep, does not forget, and does not invoice you at the end of the month.

This matters because small businesses cannot hire a finance person, a marketer, an operations manager, and a security officer. But they still need all of those jobs done. Hermes is the compromise that is not a compromise.
