## Real Pain Points (from public sources)

1. **Razor-thin margins and rising costs.** A Houstonia 50th-anniversary piece notes that founder Karl Kilian built the store as a literary haven; co-owner Matt Henneman explains that a group of 14 investors kept it afloat during the big-box and Amazon disruption. Today the store competes with online giants on price while paying urban rent.
2. **Inventory management in a small footprint.** The sales floor is only ~2,800 sq ft with a 700 sq ft back room. The buyer must carefully choose which titles, sidelines, and gifts earn their limited shelf space.
3. **Price competition.** Yelp reviews praise the curation but note that prices are at or above retail, pushing some shoppers to Amazon or Barnes & Noble. One reviewer said they would "rather just go to Barnes & Nobles or Amazon for that."
4. **Event coordination and community volatility.** Events drive traffic and loyalty but require author logistics, promotion, seating, and staff coverage. Brookline.News-style community tensions can also disrupt operations.
5. **Staff attentiveness.** While many reviews highlight friendly, knowledgeable staff, some Yelp reviews describe inattentive or brusque interactions that hurt the in-store experience.

## Why Partenon Fits

- **Scribe** can track COGS, event costs, shipping, and rent as a percentage of revenue, and flag low-margin categories (books vs. gifts vs. events).
- **Strategist** can coordinate event calendars, restocking schedules, supplier deliveries, and seasonal promotions.
- **Herald** can plan monthly event and reading calendars, local SEO copy, and newsletter content tied to staff picks.
- **Collector** can handle online orders, event ticket links, and loyalty subscriptions through Stripe.
- **Diplomat** can manage publisher and author relationships, event confirmations, and community partnerships.
- **Brain** can index buying decisions and customer preferences so recommendations stay consistent across staff shifts.
