# Company Card: Buffer

**Business type:** Bootstrapped SaaS / social media management platform  
**Location:** Fully distributed; founded in San Francisco, CA, USA  
**Founded:** 2010  
**Approximate size:** 70 team members per Buffer’s own August 2025 shareholder update; third-party directories estimate up to ~226  
**Website:** https://buffer.com

---

## Services and products

- Social media publishing and scheduling across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, X, Threads, Bluesky, YouTube, Pinterest, Mastodon, and Facebook
- Content creation tools, AI assistant, and content idea storage
- Community engagement / comment management
- Analytics and reporting dashboards
- Team collaboration features for agencies and SMBs

---

## Rough revenue estimate

Buffer publishes transparent shareholder updates. As of **August 2025**:

- **MRR:** $1,854,462
- **ARR:** $22,253,545
- **Customers:** 65,815
- **Team size:** 70
- **Net income:** $296,544 (August 2025)
- **Bank balance:** $3,585,565

Earlier estimates (GetLatka, ChartMogul) placed revenue between $20M and $31M. Buffer has raised only ~$4M historically.

---

## Real pain points (from public sources)

1. **Churn and retention.** Buffer changed its MRR/ARR calculation to recognize cancellations immediately, stating that “recurring revenue should show what we can expect going forward.” That move suggests retention is a core operational focus.
2. **ARPU pressure.** ChartMogul noted Buffer’s average revenue per customer at around $25, and the company deliberately focused on ARPA growth to sustain MRR while customer count stayed flat.
3. **Competition from platform-native tools.** Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, and X continue to build native scheduling and analytics, pressuring Buffer’s differentiation and pricing power.
4. **Support scaling across time zones.** Buffer emphasizes human, worldwide support; as a distributed team, maintaining response quality without inflating headcount is a recurring ops challenge.
5. **Feature prioritization.** Buffer launched highly requested features such as Bulk Upload, but each addition requires clear ROI analysis against a lean team.
