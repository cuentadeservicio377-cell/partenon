# Company Card: Tally

## Business type
Bootstrapped B2B SaaS / no-code form builder

## Real business name
Tally (tally.so)

## Location
Remote-first, based in Belgium. Co-founders Marie Martens and Filip Minev are based in Europe. The company serves a global user base.

## Approximate size / employees
Small independent team. The company was run by two co-founders for the first couple of years and has since grown to roughly 8–10 people while staying bootstrapped.

## Services / products
- No-code form builder for surveys, applications, registrations, polls, and payments
- Freemium model with unlimited free forms and responses
- Tally Pro subscription for custom branding, custom domains, logic, integrations, and removing the "Made with Tally" badge
- Integrations with Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets, Stripe, Zapier, Make, and others
- Templates and a community for sharing forms

## Rough revenue estimate
Publicly shared on the company blog. Tally crossed $5M ARR in April 2026, with MRR growing from $358K in January 2026 to $422K in April 2026. Earlier milestones include $100K MRR in February 2024 and $2M ARR in February 2025.

## Real pain points from public sources
1. **Founder bandwidth across product, marketing, and operations**. With a tiny team, Marie handles marketing, operations, support, and administration while Filip builds the product. Time is the main constraint.
2. **Subscription billing and churn**. Tally relies on a small percentage of free users converting to Pro. Tracking MRR, churn, failed payments, and plan upgrades is essential.
3. **Support and documentation at scale**. Hundreds of thousands of users need help, but the team is small. The help center and support boundaries must be clear.
4. **Security and abuse monitoring**. As a form platform, Tally must block abusive forms, manage API keys, and keep integrations secure.
5. **Content distribution and SEO**. The company grows mostly through organic channels, product-led growth, and community. Consistent content and distribution are critical but compete with product work.
