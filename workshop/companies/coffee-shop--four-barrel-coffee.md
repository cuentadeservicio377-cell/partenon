# Four Barrel Coffee — Company Card

**Business type:** Coffee shop / specialty roaster
**Location:** 375 Valencia Street, San Francisco, CA 94103, USA
**Founded:** 2007 (opened first cafe on Valencia Street)
**Approximate size:** 3 San Francisco cafes + wholesale accounts; roughly 50–100 employees (industry estimate based on multi-location roastery/retail operation)
**Website:** https://www.fourbarrelcoffee.com

## Services and products

- Single-origin and blended specialty coffee, roasted in-house
- Espresso drinks, pour-over, batch brew, and cold beverages
- Whole-bean retail sales, subscriptions, and wholesale to other cafes
- Light pastries and food pairings
- Coffee education and public cuppings

## Rough revenue estimate

Private company; no public financials. Comparable independent multi-location roasters in the Bay Area with similar footprint and wholesale volume typically report annual revenue in the **$5M–$15M** range. Four Barrel's 2021 SBA 504 loan financed a combined **$5.4M** in real estate and roastery project costs, indicating a business of at least mid-seven-figure scale.

## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Margin pressure from rent and real-estate risk.** Co-owner Jodi Geren told TMC Financing the team was "really getting nervous that we'd be priced out of the neighborhoods we helped establish when our leases are up." The SBA loan allowed Four Barrel to purchase its 9th Street building to secure a permanent San Francisco presence.
2. **Supply-cost volatility and pricing transparency.** A 2018 San Francisco Chronicle report noted that popular local roasters like Four Barrel were raising prices due to decreasing supply and rising production costs, and the company organized a forum to explain price increases to wholesale customers.
3. **Labor and workplace culture.** The same Chronicle investigation documented sexual harassment allegations against founder Jeremy Tooker and described a workplace where problematic behavior had been "normalized." Tooker left the company in 2018; the case illustrates how fast-growing independent hospitality businesses can outrun HR and compliance systems.
4. **Wholesale scaling complexity.** Retail initially drove the business, but wholesale became the growth driver. Managing a growing roster of wholesale accounts alongside three retail locations adds inventory, delivery, and customer-service overhead.
