# Plausible Analytics — Company Card

**Business type:** Bootstrapped B2B SaaS / privacy-first web analytics

**Real business name:** Plausible Analytics

**Location:** Tartu, Estonia (fully remote team)

**Founded:** 2019

**Approximate size:** Approximately 8 employees

**Website:** https://plausible.io

## Services and products

- Privacy-first, cookieless web analytics platform
- Open-source analytics script under 1.4 KB
- GDPR and CCPA compliance by default
- Hosted cloud service and self-hosted Community Edition (AGPL v3)
- Subscription pricing based on monthly pageviews
- Public metrics and transparent company updates

## Rough revenue estimate

Private bootstrapped company. GetLatka estimates **$3.1M ARR** in 2024, up from $2.1M in 2023. Plausible publishes monthly recurring revenue updates publicly and has not raised outside funding.

## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Resource constraints against well-funded competitors.** Plausible competes with Google Analytics, Fathom, Matomo, and PostHog. Without venture capital, every engineering, support, and marketing hour must be allocated deliberately.

2. **Support scaling.** With roughly 8 employees serving 15,000+ paying subscribers, documentation and self-service become essential, but edge-case questions still pull founder time.

3. **Feature velocity vs. simplicity.** The product's positioning is radical simplicity, yet users request deeper reporting, funnel analysis, and platform integrations. Saying no is part of the strategy, but it creates churn pressure.

4. **Churn and retention.** Average revenue per user is modest (many customers on lower tiers). Retention, expansion revenue, and failed-payment recovery matter more than new-logo growth alone.

5. **Infrastructure and compliance costs.** Serving billions of pageviews on EU-owned infrastructure while maintaining uptime and responding to evolving privacy regulation is an operational cost that grows with usage.
