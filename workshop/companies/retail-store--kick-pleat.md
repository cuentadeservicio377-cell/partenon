# Company Card: Kick Pleat

**Business type:** Independent designer clothing boutique (women's and men's)  
**Location:** Austin, TX, USA (North Lamar corridor)  
**Founded:** 2003  
**Approximate size:** 8-50 employees across retail, e-commerce, and buying operations  
**Website:** https://kickpleat.com  

---

## Services and products

- Curated minimalist and modern clothing, jewelry, and shoes
- In-store styling and personalized customer service
- E-commerce with national shipping
- Designer brands and house lines (e.g., WP Martin)

---

## Rough revenue estimate

Private company. Revenue is split between physical store traffic and e-commerce. Boutique apparel margins are pressured by inventory carrying costs, markdowns, and return shipping.

---

## Real pain points (from public sources)

1. **Slow online shipping.** A WorthEPenny review states shipping was "way slower than expected," which hurts repeat e-commerce purchases.
2. **Mixed customer ratings.** Wanderlog aggregates a 3.7/5 score with several one-star reviews, indicating service or fulfillment variance that can affect brand perception.
3. **Inventory sync across channels.** Boutiques must track size/color variants across the physical store and website. Disconnected inventory leads to stockouts, overselling, and manual reconciliation.
4. **Seasonal markdowns and dead stock.** Holding unsold inventory ties up capital and floor space. Markdowns without sell-through data erode margin.
5. **Returns and customer relationship management.** The shop uses Loop Returns, suggesting returns volume is material. Without centralized customer history, personalized follow-up and loyalty are hard.
