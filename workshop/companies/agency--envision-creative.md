## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Cash-flow volatility from project-based work.** Agency revenue depends on retainers and project cycles. Losing or delaying one major client creates an immediate gap because payroll and media spend continue regardless.

2. **Scope creep and margin erosion.** The agency sells strategy, creative, and media execution together. Without strict scoping and change-order discipline, extra rounds of revisions and unplanned deliverables can make engagements unprofitable.

3. **Client retention and pipeline consistency.** Reviews praise creative quality and data analysis, but agencies of this size live on a small number of retainers. Pipeline visibility and follow-up discipline are critical.

4. **Operational overhead without dedicated finance staff.** Account managers and founders often handle billing, forecasting, and collections while also selling and delivering work.

5. **Reporting transparency across channels.** Clients expect clear attribution across SEO, PPC, social, and email. Aggregating and explaining those numbers takes time that is rarely billable.
