# Greenlight Bookstore — Company Card

**Business type:** Independent bookstore and retail (jointly operated with Yours Truly, Brooklyn stationery)

**Real business name:** Greenlight Bookstore

**Location:** 686 Fulton Street, Brooklyn, NY 11217, USA

**Founded:** 2006

**Approximate size:** Approximately 40 workers across Greenlight Bookstore and Yours Truly, Brooklyn (RWDSU bargaining unit)

**Website:** https://www.greenlightbookstore.com

## Services and products

- New books across all genres
- Author readings, book clubs, story times, and community events
- Staff recommendations and curated bestseller lists
- Stationery, cards, gifts, and home goods via Yours Truly, Brooklyn
- Bookstore-union partnership and community programming

## Rough revenue estimate

Private company; no audited public financials. Industry estimates for a well-known independent bookstore of this size in a high-rent New York City location typically place annual revenue in the **$3M–$8M** range.

## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Razor-thin margins and rising labor costs.** Independent bookstores operate on low single-digit margins. Greenlight's 2025 union contract includes 11% wage increases over three years and a 5% annual increase in employer healthcare contributions, which directly pressure the bottom line.

2. **Competition from online giants and e-books.** Amazon, larger chains, and digital alternatives have impacted independent bookstores for years, forcing differentiation through experience, curation, and community events.

3. **Inventory management across multiple product types.** The business sells new books, sidelines, and gifts across two locations. Deciding which categories turn fastest and which tie up capital is a constant optimization problem.

4. **Complex scheduling.** The union contract requires a minimum of three employees for all opening and closing shifts, adding discipline to a workforce that includes part-time and seasonal booksellers.

5. **Event-driven volatility.** Author events drive foot traffic but also create uncertainty around attendance, staffing, and incremental sales.
