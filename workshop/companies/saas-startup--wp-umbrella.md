## Real Pain Points (from public year-in-review and founder posts)

1. **Messy multi-currency reconciliation.** The founder notes that currency variation alone cost roughly €7,000 per month, and Stripe fees totaled roughly €40,000 over the year once the company crossed $100K MRR.
2. **Subscription and payment failures.** As the customer base grows, failed payments, refunds, and churn signals become a real operational load for a small team.
3. **Security and API key hygiene.** The product manages thousands of client WordPress sites; credentials, API keys, and access logs must be audited and rotated.
4. **Knowledge transfer at small-team scale.** With only seven people, decisions about pricing, positioning, backups, and reliability must be indexed so new hires catch up fast.
5. **Operational consolidation vs. feature velocity.** The team spent significant time in 2025 making existing infrastructure reliable rather than shipping visible features, which requires strong operational visibility.

## Why Partenon Fits

- **Guardian** can audit API keys, Stripe tokens, server credentials, and access policies from day one.
- **Scribe** can track cloud infrastructure, contractor, and tool spend against runway and MRR.
- **Collector** can manage Stripe subscriptions, failed-payment retries, refunds, and weekly revenue reports.
- **Strategist** can run sprints, incident post-mortems, and hiring pipelines.
- **Brain** can index decisions about pricing, backup architecture, and positioning so the team does not re-litigate them.
- **Herald** can draft changelog updates, founder updates, and community content for WordCamps and meetups.
