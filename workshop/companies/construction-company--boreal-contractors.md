## Real pain points (from public sources)

1. **Permit and inspection delays.** Contractors commonly face confusing city websites, missing forms, plan-review backlogs, and unclear points of contact. Each delay pushes the schedule and compresses cash flow.
2. **Subcontractor insurance and COI tracking.** A Phoenix-area general contractor discovered a $2.3 million problem when a subcontractor's workers' compensation policy had expired. Manual certificate-of-insurance tracking guarantees coverage gaps.
3. **Schedule and change-order pressure.** Missed material deliveries, trade coordination failures, and owner-driven changes cascade into delays, claims, and late fees.
4. **Vague specifications and communication gaps.** Home-remodeling red-flag guides warn that vague project specs and poor communication patterns predict cost overruns and disputes.
5. **Cash flow tied to milestones.** Material and labor costs are paid ahead of progress billing. Any delay in owner payment or inspection sign-off strains working capital.
