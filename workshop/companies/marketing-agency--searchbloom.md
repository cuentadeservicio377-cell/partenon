## Real Pain Points (from public sources)

1. **Scope control across retainer clients.** Clutch reviews are overwhelmingly positive, but one verified reviewer in the construction sector noted the team "executed the plan but has communicated poorly at times" — a classic agency signal when project scope and status updates outrun internal operations.
2. **Planning-stage pressure.** A Clutch highlight notes Searchbloom "could benefit from spending more time in the planning stage." Planning gaps turn into rework, deadline pressure, and margin erosion.
3. **Multi-channel reporting overhead.** Clients expect clear attribution across SEO, PPC, content, and CRO. Consolidating numbers into client-ready reports consumes senior analyst hours.
4. **Talent retention and capacity planning.** Utah agency talent is competitive; headcount estimates vary across directories, suggesting contractor blending or turnover that complicates utilization tracking.
5. **Direct analyst access at scale.** Searchbloom sells direct analyst access as a differentiator. As client count grows, preserving that access without overloading individuals requires tight task and milestone tracking.

## Why Partenon Fits

- **Strategist** can create a standard retainer project template with phases (discovery, audit, creative, launch, optimization, reporting) and assign owners/due dates.
- **Diplomat** can register every prospect and client, log sales calls, and run daily follow-ups so proposals do not stall.
- **Scribe** can match retainer revenue to delivery hours, freelancer costs, and software spend per client, flagging over-serviced accounts.
- **Herald** can turn closed wins into case-study copy and LinkedIn posts, checked against banned-claim rules.
- **Collector** can invoice monthly retainers, project deposits, and follow up on overdue accounts.
- **Brain** can index winning proposal templates, positioning decisions, and campaign learnings so new analysts onboard faster.
