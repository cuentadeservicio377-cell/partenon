## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Job costing and material price volatility.** Small contractors must estimate materials, labor, and subcontractors accurately. Lumber, steel, and fuel price swings can erase margins on fixed-price bids.

2. **Cash-flow timing and retainage.** Projects require upfront material purchases and labor before the customer pays progress draws. Retainage held until closeout creates working-capital gaps that strain a small back office.

3. **Subcontractor scheduling and coordination.** On-time delivery depends on coordinating multiple trades. One missed delivery or permit delay cascades through the schedule.

4. **Bonding and insurance compliance.** Public and commercial work often requires bonds, general liability, and workers' compensation. Tracking expiration dates and coverage limits is essential but easy to miss.

5. **Sales-to-operations handoff.** Winning a bid is only the start. Consistent lead tracking, quotes, contracts, and scheduling handoffs are needed to turn estimates into profitable projects.

## Why Partenon fits

- **Strategist** can create a project and checklist for every job (pre-construction, construction, closeout), assign owners and due dates, and escalate blocked tasks such as permit delays.
- **Diplomat** can register clients, log site visits and change orders, and run daily follow-ups so prospects do not go cold.
- **Scribe** can cost each project separately (materials, labor, subcontractors, permits) and compare actuals against the estimate.
- **Collector** can generate payment links or invoices for deposits and progress draws, and follow up on overdue balances.
- **Guardian** can secure access to CAD files, contractor portals, and company social accounts, and rotate keys when subcontractors or staff change.
