# Company Card: Coffee By Design

## Business type
Independent coffee roaster and cafe chain

## Real business name
Coffee By Design

## Location
Portland, Maine, United States. Four coffeehouses in the Greater Portland area (Diamond Street roastery/cafe, Congress Street, Freeport, and other retail spots), plus a craft roastery at 1 Diamond Street.

## Approximate size / employees
Roughly 55 employees (reported when the company won the Maine SBA Small Business Person of the Year award). Operates four cafes and sells wholesale to nearly 600 accounts.

## Services / products
- Handcrafted espresso, drip coffee, cold brew, and seasonal beverages
- In-house roasted whole-bean coffee sold retail, online, and wholesale
- Cafe food, pastries, and merch
- Coffee sourcing trips and direct-trade relationships with growers
- Community events and local arts support (First Friday Artwalk, Rebel Blend donations)

## Rough revenue estimate
Private company; exact revenue is not public. Based on 55 employees, four locations, a 45,000 sq ft roastery/headquarters, and ~600 wholesale accounts, estimated annual revenue is likely in the low-to-mid seven-figure USD range.

## Real pain points from public sources
1. **Labor and scheduling complexity**. Running four cafe locations plus a roastery means shift scheduling, payroll, and coverage are daily puzzles. Founder Mary Allen Lindemann describes her role as "personnel management" and "head cheerleader," signaling how much time leadership spends on people operations.
2. **Tight margins on a multi-channel operation**. Wholesale, retail, online, and cafe sales each have different cost structures. Without clean numbers, it is hard to know which channel is actually profitable.
3. **Inventory and supplier coordination**. Green coffee is bought in bulk through direct-trade relationships with lead times and currency exposure. Roasted beans and retail merchandise must be tracked across four locations plus shipping.
4. **Marketing consistency across channels**. The brand is built on community, sustainability, and local arts, but keeping social content, event calendars, and wholesale customer communication consistent is hard with a small team.
5. **Founder dependency**. Lindemann bought out her co-founder and now owns 100% of the company. Much of the strategy, sourcing, marketing, and community relationships sit with her, which creates operational risk.

## Why Partenon fits
- **Scribe** can build a margin dashboard that separates cafe, wholesale, online, and retail merchandise revenue and costs.
- **Strategist** can own the staff schedule, roasting calendar, and supplier lead times so the founder is not the only person who knows what is happening.
- **Herald** can generate a weekly content calendar tied to events, seasonal blends, and community partnerships.
- **Collector** can set up Stripe payment links for online merch, event tickets, and catering deposits.
- **Diplomat** can track wholesale accounts, event sponsors, and vendor relationships with milestones and follow-ups.
- **Guardian** can manage credentials for the POS, e-commerce platform, and wholesale portal.
