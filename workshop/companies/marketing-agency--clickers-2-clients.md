Small digital marketing agency

Very small team: owner Rebecca Van Diest, an executive assistant, a website developer, an SEO specialist, and an automated virtual assistant named "Ava." Estimated 3–5 human employees plus contractors.

- Website design and management

- Email marketing
- Social media management
- Video editing

## Rough revenue estimate
Private company; revenue is not public. A small agency with a 3–5 person team and local SMB clients typically ranges from low-six-figures to mid-six-figures USD annually, depending on retainer mix and project volume.

## Real pain points from public sources
1. **Juggling client work and business operations**. The owner started in digital marketing in 2019 and handles research, design, strategy, and client management. Small agencies often lack a dedicated operations person.
2. **No structured project management visible**. Services span SEO, websites, social, email, and video. Without a shared project system, deadlines and client commitments can slip.
3. **Client acquisition and follow-up**. The agency promises to help clients "Show Up and Stand Out." That requires consistent prospecting, proposals, and follow-ups, which compete with delivery time.
4. **Reporting and proof of ROI**. Clients want to see results, but reporting is often manual and spread across Google Business Profile, social dashboards, and analytics tools.
5. **Scope creep on flat-fee work**. A small team offering many services is vulnerable to clients asking for "just one more thing" without a change order process.

## Why Partenon fits
- **Strategist** can run projects, deadlines, and weekly planning so delivery does not depend on the owner's memory.
- **Diplomat** can log client interactions, track proposals, and run daily follow-up reports.
- **Scribe** can build a simple P&L by client and by service line, flagging which retainers are profitable.
- **Herald** can use its own content engine to market the agency: case studies, social posts, and newsletter copy.
- **Collector** can invoice retainers and one-off projects through Stripe and flag overdue accounts.
- **Guardian** can manage credentials for client Google accounts, social profiles, and hosting panels.
