## Real pain points (from public sources)

1. **Service inconsistency.** A Yelp review notes "Good coffee but bad customer service" and that baristas were not friendly, suggesting staffing and training directly affect repeat visits.
2. **Labor scheduling pressure.** Coffee shop labor typically runs 25-35% of revenue; overstaffing during slow periods and understaffing during peaks both hurt margins and service quality.
3. **Perishable inventory and waste.** Milk, pastries, and seasonal ingredients spoil quickly. Poor inventory tracking leads to over-ordering, stockouts, or unmeasured waste.
4. **Slim margins and cash-flow timing.** Daily revenue fluctuates with foot traffic, while rent, payroll, and supplier payments are due on fixed schedules. Visibility into daily margin is rare without disciplined tracking.
5. **Online/delivery cost pressure.** The shop appears on Favor with a $6.29 delivery fee, and online bean orders compete with in-store traffic. Third-party commissions and shipping costs can erode net margin if not priced carefully.
