## Rough revenue estimate

Private company; no audited public financials. Clutch reports project sizes ranging from **$10,000 to $300,000**, with the most common engagement at **$10,000–$49,999** based on 11 verified reviews. Industry benchmarks for a 10–49 person growth agency in New York suggest annual revenue in the **$3M–$10M** range.

## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Cash-flow volatility from project-based work.** Ladder's own clients report project sizes that vary by an order of magnitude. Agency-wide surveys cited by Finoya, Ravetree, and Hiline identify inconsistent client payments, project-based revenue models, and net-30 to net-90 payment cycles as the top financial risks for micro- and small marketing agencies.
2. **Scope creep and margin erosion.** Ladder's positioning emphasizes "quarterly plans with monthly tests," which can expand scope if test cadence and deliverables are not tightly scoped. Industry research reports that scope creep is a primary reason agency projects become unprofitable.
3. **Client retention and pipeline consistency.** Clutch reviews praise Ladder's creative quality and data analysis, but some note "project management consistency and delivery timelines" as a concern. Agencies of this size often depend on a small number of retainers; losing one can create an immediate cash gap.
4. **Operational overhead without dedicated finance staff.** Marketing agencies rarely have a CFO at this size, so budgeting, forecasting, and collections rely on founders or account managers who are also selling and delivering work.

## Why Partenon fits

- **Strategist** can track every client as a project with deadlines, owners, and dependencies, and generate agency-specific checklists for campaign launches.
- **Diplomat** can log prospects, proposals, contracts, and follow-ups so no deal slips through the cracks.
- **Scribe** can match project budgets to actual hours and vendor costs, flag accounts where work exceeds scope, and build a 13-week cash forecast.
- **Collector** can invoice retainers, create payment links for one-off projects, and follow up on overdue accounts before cash gaps appear.
