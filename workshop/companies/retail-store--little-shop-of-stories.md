# Company Card: Little Shop of Stories

## Business type
Independent children's bookstore and community events space

## Real business name
Little Shop of Stories

## Location
133A East Court Square, Decatur, Georgia, United States. Located in downtown Decatur, near Atlanta.

## Approximate size / employees
Small independent bookstore. Exact headcount is not public, but the business is run by two co-owners plus a bookselling team, event coordinators, and seasonal/camp staff. Estimated 8–15 employees.

## Services / products
- Curated children's, middle-grade, and young-adult books
- Adult books for the grownups kids become
- Author events, storytimes, and book clubs
- Summer reading programs and book-themed camps
- School book fairs (in-person and virtual)
- Community-wide reading initiative (On The Same Page)
- Decatur Children's Book Festival
- Online ordering and local delivery

## Rough revenue estimate
Private company; revenue is not public. A single-location independent bookstore with strong event programming and school partnerships typically generates mid-six-figures to low-seven-figures USD annually.

## Real pain points from public sources
1. **Inventory management across channels**. The store must balance physical shelf space, online orders, school book fairs, event sales, and camp merchandise. During the pandemic it essentially became an online fulfillment center.
2. **Event-driven revenue uncertainty**. Author events, festivals, and camps are high-impact but date-dependent. One weather issue or supply-chain delay can move revenue between months.
3. **School and institutional partnerships**. Managing book fairs, school orders, and nonprofit partnerships requires tracking contacts, deadlines, invoices, and follow-ups.
4. **Community content and marketing**. The store's brand is built on enthusiasm, events, and literacy advocacy. Keeping the newsletter, social media, and event calendar current is a constant job.
5. **Thin retail margins and cash flow**. Book retail margins are narrow. Managing COGS, returns, and seasonal cash flow is critical.

## Why Partenon fits
- **Scribe** can track sales by channel (store, online, book fairs, events, camps) and flag margin by category.
- **Strategist** can manage the event calendar, camp schedules, and school-partnership deadlines.
- **Herald** can produce a weekly content calendar for events, staff picks, and community partnerships.
- **Collector** can handle online orders, event tickets, and camp registrations through Stripe payment links.
- **Diplomat** can maintain relationships with schools, authors, sponsors, and community organizations.
- **Brain** can index learnings from each event and festival to improve the next one.
