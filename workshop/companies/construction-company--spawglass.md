# Company Card: SpawGlass

**Business type:** Commercial construction / general contracting  
**Location:** Texas, USA (offices in Austin, San Antonio, New Braunfels, and other Texas markets)  
**Founded:** 1953  
**Approximate size:** 25–100 employees; estimated annual revenue $25M–$100M  
**Website:** https://www.spawglass.com

---

## Services and products

- General contracting and construction management for commercial, education, healthcare, and industrial projects
- Virtual design and construction (VDC) / BIM services
- Preconstruction, estimating, and scheduling
- Self-perform capabilities and subcontractor coordination
- Safety, quality, and sustainability programs

---

## Rough revenue estimate

Owler estimates **$25M–$100M annual revenue** with 25–100 employees. Commercial contractors in this band typically run on single-digit net margins, so cost control and schedule adherence are critical.

---

## Real pain points (from public sources)

1. **Project schedule risk.** News coverage notes SpawGlass broke ground on a new elementary school in Beeville, Texas, and has completed senior-living and university projects; each public deadline creates material-delivery and subcontractor coordination pressure.
2. **Subcontractor and supplier coordination.** General contractors rely on dozens of trade partners; missed deliveries or change orders cascade into delays and claims.
3. **Safety and compliance documentation.** Construction carries high insurance and OSHA exposure; daily logs, incident reports, and training records must be auditable.
4. **Thin margins and change-order tracking.** Revenue per employee is modest compared with professional services; accurate job costing separates profitable projects from losses.
5. **Business development follow-through.** Pursuing public and private work requires proposal deadlines, prequalification milestones, and relationship follow-up.
