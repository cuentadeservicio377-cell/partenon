# Brookline Booksmith — Company Card

**Business type:** Independent bookstore / retail
**Location:** 279 Harvard Street, Brookline, MA 02446, USA
**Founded:** 1961
**Approximate size:** Single-location independent bookstore with used-book cellar and gift sections; roughly 20–50 employees (industry estimate for a store of its size and event programming)
**Website:** https://www.brooklinebooksmith.com

## Services and products

- New and used books across all genres
- Curated gifts, cards, stationery, candles, and home goods
- Author readings, book clubs, story times, and community events
- Staff recommendations and curated bestseller lists
- Banned-books and social-activism programming

## Rough revenue estimate

Private company; no audited public financials. Harvard Business School research on independent bookstores notes that profit margins are "in the low single digits" and that successful stores diversify revenue through sidelines and events. Industry estimates for a well-known independent bookstore of this size in a high-rent Boston suburb typically place annual revenue in the **$3M–$8M** range.

## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Razor-thin margins and rising costs.** A Harvard Business School case study identifies "living wages, rising rents, and razor-thin profit margins" as the three primary external challenges facing independent bookstores. Brookline Booksmith co-owner Jed Smith has publicly discussed the store's 64-year history of adapting to cultural and economic shifts while remaining community-focused.
2. **Competition from online giants and chain retailers.** Co-owner Win (quoted in the Boston College Heights) stated that Amazon, larger chain bookstores, and e-books have impacted the store over the years, forcing independent bookstores to differentiate through experience and curation.
3. **Inventory management across multiple product types.** The store sells new books, used books, and a wide array of gift merchandise. Managing which categories turn fastest, which tie up capital, and which drive foot traffic is a constant optimization problem.
4. **Community-driven volatility.** Brookline.News reporting in 2025 described how the store became a flashpoint for local protests after hosting author events, illustrating how community-focused retailers can face reputational and operational disruptions unrelated to core commerce.
