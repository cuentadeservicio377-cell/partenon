# Oblique Coffee Roasters — Company Card

**Business type:** Independent coffee roaster and cafe

**Real business name:** Oblique Coffee Roasters

**Location:** 3039 SE Stark Street, Portland, OR 97214, USA

**Founded:** 2010

**Approximate size:** 1–10 employees; owner-operated with a small crew

**Website:** https://obliquecoffeeroasters.com

## Services and products

- Espresso drinks, drip coffee, cold brew, chai, matcha, and tea
- On-site roasted whole-bean coffee sold retail, online, and to-go
- Cafe food and pastries
- Community events and live music
- Bags of single-origin and blended coffees

## Rough revenue estimate

Private company. Prospeo estimates annual revenue at approximately **$85,555** and revenue per employee at about $86,000. The business has not raised outside funding.

## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Building and family asset risk.** The historic Landauer Mercantile Building (built 1891) houses both the cafe and the owner's family home. A divorce settlement required a $120,000 buyout to avoid liquidation of the property, and a public fundraiser was organized to retire debt and save the business.

2. **Owner dependency and limited hours.** The founder works seven days a week, and hours have been scaled back to 9am–3pm because of family scheduling needs. This limits revenue capacity and operational redundancy.

3. **Historic building maintenance costs.** The building was previously designated structurally unsound and required a multi-year renovation. Ongoing maintenance and preservation obligations compete with daily cash flow.

4. **Tight margins and local competition.** As a small independent roaster in a city with many coffee options, Oblique competes on atmosphere and quality rather than price, leaving little room for waste or payroll inefficiency.

5. **No formal finance back office.** With a skeleton crew, bookkeeping, vendor payments, and cash-flow forecasting rely on the owner's attention alongside roasting and service.
