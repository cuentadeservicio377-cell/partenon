## Real Pain Points (from public sources)

1. **Project scheduling and subcontractor coordination.** Marsh Bell's website emphasizes on-time, on-budget delivery, but commercial construction lives or dies by trade sequencing. One missed electrical or plumbing milestone cascades into drywall, flooring, and closeout delays.
2. **Change-order and cost tracking.** Design-build work involves ongoing scope decisions. Without real-time cost tracking, change orders erode margin before the project closes.
3. **Cash-flow timing.** Construction payment cycles are slow; subcontractors and suppliers must be paid before client retainers and progress payments arrive. A $17.5M Greer project announced in 2026 is large relative to the company's reported revenue, amplifying working-capital pressure.
4. **Document and decision scattering.** Material selections, permits, site photos, and client approvals live in email, text messages, and site visits, making it hard to reconstruct project status.
5. **Repeat-client relationship management.** The company states that 80% of work comes from repeat clients or referrals. Maintaining those relationships requires systematic follow-up and milestone tracking.

## Why Partenon Fits

- **Strategist** can create phase checklists for each project (pre-construction, construction, closeout) and assign owners and due dates.
- **Diplomat** can track suppliers, subcontractors, contracts, and confirmation milestones.
- **Scribe** can cost each project separately (materials, labor, equipment, subcontractors, permits) and compare actual spend to the estimate weekly.
- **Guardian** can manage shared accounts for supplier portals, CAD/modeling tools, and insurance systems.
- **Collector** can invoice progress payments and retainers, then follow up on overdue accounts.
- **Brain** can index lessons learned per project type (healthcare, industrial, institutional) so estimates improve over time.
