## Real pain points from public sources

1. **Project scheduling and subcontractor coordination.** D&M's site emphasizes "on-time arrivals" and "consistent contact" as differentiators, which suggests these are common failure points in the local market. Construction research consistently cites poor communication, scheduling delays, and trade coordination as top project risks.
2. **Cash-flow timing and payment terms.** Residential remodels and new builds require upfront material purchases, labor, and subcontractor payments before the customer pays final draws. The Buildxact "State of Residential Construction" report and industry guides note that slow customer payments and working-capital gaps are leading causes of stress for small builders.
3. **Scope creep and change orders.** Kitchen/bath remodels and custom home builds are prone to customer-driven changes. Without clear project checklists and change-order discipline, margins erode quickly.
4. **Sales-to-operations handoff.** The company markets a simple three-step process (message, measure, build). Scaling that process requires consistent lead tracking, quotes, contracts, and scheduling handoffs that many small contractors manage ad hoc.

## Why Partenon fits

- **Strategist** can create a project and checklist for every job (pre-construction, construction, closeout), assign owners and due dates, and escalate blocked tasks such as permit delays.
- **Diplomat** can register clients, log site visits and change orders, and run daily follow-ups so prospects do not go cold.
- **Scribe** can cost each project separately (materials, labor, subcontractors, permits) and compare actuals against the estimate.
- **Collector** can generate payment links or invoices for deposits and progress draws, and follow up on overdue balances.
- **Guardian** can secure access to CAD files, contractor portals, and company social accounts, and rotate keys when subcontractors or staff change.
