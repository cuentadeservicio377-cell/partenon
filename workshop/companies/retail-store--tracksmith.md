# Company Card: Tracksmith

**Business type:** Athletic apparel / performance running brand (direct-to-consumer retail)  
**Location:** Boston, Massachusetts, USA; retail stores in Boston, New York, and London  
**Founded:** 2014  
**Approximate size:** 55 employees per Owler; 11–50 per ZoomInfo; ~20 per Kona Equity  
**Website:** https://www.tracksmith.com

---

## Services and products

- Premium running apparel, shoes, and accessories for men and women
- Direct-to-consumer e-commerce and flagship retail stores
- The Twilight 5000 race series and community running events
- Limited-edition collections and collaborations (e.g., Paris Olympics Federation Collection)
- Content and storytelling around the “amateur spirit” of running

---

## Rough revenue estimate

Sources vary: Owler estimates **$8.3M annual revenue**, Kona Equity reports **$5.7M**, and ECDB estimates **$25M in 2025 GMV** for tracksmith.com alone. Tracksmith has raised approximately $5.8M in funding.

---

## Real pain points (from public sources)

1. **Conversion-rate gap.** Grips Intelligence reported tracksmith.com at a 1.00–1.50% conversion rate in April 2026, below the sports-industry high benchmark of 5.27%, indicating room for merchandising and site-optimization work.
2. **Seasonal inventory and cash tied up in stock.** As a premium apparel brand with frequent drops, Tracksmith must balance limited production with demand forecasting; too much inventory hurts cash flow, too little loses sales.
3. **High customer-acquisition costs.** Competing against Nike, Hoka, On, and other well-funded brands requires paid social, influencer, and event marketing with disciplined ROI tracking.
4. **Multi-channel operations.** E-commerce, wholesale, pop-ups, and owned retail stores each have different cost structures and fulfillment requirements.
5. **International expansion complexity.** Opening the Paris pop-up and London store adds currency, logistics, and local marketing coordination.
